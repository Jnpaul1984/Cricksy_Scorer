## Session 2026-05-09 — Thinking Like a Cricket Analyst

### Curriculum Location

- Level: Beginner Cricket Analyst
- Course: Cricket Foundations
- Module: Cricket System Fundamentals
- Lesson: Introduction to Cricket Formats

### Session Objectives

- [x] Understand the difference between fan watching and analyst watching.
- [x] Understand why cricket context matters before judging an event.
- [x] Identify dot-ball pressure as a tactical pattern.
- [x] Connect momentum detection to a future Cricksy AI feature.

### Theory Covered

- Analysts ask context-driven questions instead of making surface-level judgments.
- Match context includes score, run rate, wickets, pressure, match phase, and tactical intent.
- Dot balls can create pressure and force risk, especially in T20 cricket.
- Momentum can shift because of wickets, scoring slowdown, pressure buildup, or tactical control.

### Real-World Example Discussed

- T20 pressure sequence: dot balls leading to a forced attacking shot and wicket.
- ODI example: team moving from 120/1 after 25 overs to 138/4 after 30 overs, showing momentum shifting toward the bowling side.

### Analyst Workflow Practiced

- Identify format.
- Identify context.
- Identify pressure.
- Explain the cause-and-effect pattern using: Because X happened, the pressure changed, which caused Y.

### AI-Assisted Analysis Connection

- Cricksy could detect momentum swings by analyzing pressure indicators from either the batting or bowling team.
- Useful signals include dot balls, wickets, boundary events, run-rate changes, and defensive tactical reactions.

### Cricksy Feature Connection

- Future feature: Momentum Swing Detector.
- Future feature: Pressure Build-Up Alerts.
- Future feature: Tactical response summaries after key events such as early boundaries or wicket clusters.

### Exercise / Practical Task

- Task: Answer five analyst mindset questions.
- Completion Status: Completed.
- Evidence: Student answered all five questions in-session.

### Assessment Questions / Task

1. What is the difference between how a fan watches cricket and how an analyst watches cricket?
2. In T20 cricket, why can dot balls be so powerful?
3. ODI scenario: Team is 120/1 after 25 overs and 138/4 after 30 overs. What changed in the match?
4. Give one example of something Cricksy could automatically detect from a match.
5. Complete the sentence: Because ______ happened, the pressure changed, which caused ______.

- Correctly explained that fans make surface-level judgments, while analysts ask contextual questions such as score, run rate, and batter pressure.
- Correctly identified that dot balls are powerful in T20 because they build pressure.
- Correctly interpreted the ODI scenario as momentum shifting to the bowling team because wickets slowed the batting side and forced a change in plan.
- Correctly identified momentum swings as a useful Cricksy AI detection opportunity.
- Provided a valid cause-and-effect example: an early boundary changed pressure and forced the bowling team to become defensive.
